## Artist Statement

**These works explore:**
- Limits of Unicode rendering
- Beauty in system failures
- Minimal vs. maximal expression
- Text as material with properties
- Collaboration and community
- Meta-commentary on medium itself

**Experimental art asks:**
- What happens when we break the rules?
- Where are the boundaries of "text art"?
- Can glitches be beautiful?
- What cannot be represented in Unicode?

**Viewer participation:**
- Which pieces render properly for you?
- Which break?
- Is broken rendering a failure or a feature?
- What does this reveal about your system?

## Creating Your Own Experiments

### Prompts:

1. **Use only one Unicode range** (e.g., only Egyptian Hieroglyphs)
2. **Maximum density**: Pack as much as possible
3. **Minimum viable**: What's the least needed for recognition?
4. **Intentionally break** something
5. **Combine incompatible scripts** (LTR + RTL + vertical)
6. **Use characters no one has fonts for**
7. **Create animation without animation** (suggestion of movement)
8. **Make art that only exists in specific terminals**

### Share Your Experiments

Submit experimental works to this repository via pull request. Include:
- The work itself
- Artist statement (what were you trying to do?)
- Technical notes (known rendering issues)
- Viewing requirements (font, terminal, OS)
