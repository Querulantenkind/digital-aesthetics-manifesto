# ASCII Art Exhibitions

Curated collections of ASCII art organized by theme, technique, period, or concept.

## About These Exhibitions

Each exhibition in this directory presents ASCII art through a specific lens—exploring techniques, historical periods, cultural contexts, or conceptual frameworks. These are not comprehensive surveys but carefully selected works that illuminate particular aspects of ASCII art practice.

## Current Exhibitions

### [Terminal Landscapes](01-terminal-landscapes.md)
Natural and urban landscapes rendered in ASCII. Exploring how limited character sets can evoke space, depth, and atmosphere.

### [Portraits in Monochrome](02-portraits-in-monochrome.md)
Human faces and figures in ASCII. The challenge of capturing personality, expression, and likeness with text characters.

### [Abstract Geometries](03-abstract-geometries.md)
Non-representational ASCII art. Pattern, rhythm, and visual mathematics using characters as pure form.

### [The Golden Age: 1990s ASCII](04-golden-age-1990s.md)
Historical survey of ASCII art from BBS culture, early internet, and underground scenes of the 1990s.

### [Text as Architecture](05-text-as-architecture.md)
Structural ASCII art—buildings, monuments, machines. How text can describe built forms.

### [Animated ASCII](06-animated-ascii.md)
Moving pictures in text. Animation techniques, frame-by-frame ASCII, and time-based work.

## Exhibition Format

Each exhibition includes:
- **Curatorial statement**: Concept and selection criteria
- **Featured works**: 10-20 pieces with artist attribution
- **Technical notes**: Dimensions, character sets, creation methods
- **Historical context**: When relevant
- **Further exploration**: Related artists, techniques, or themes

## Contributing to Exhibitions

Have ASCII art that fits one of these themes? See our [submission guidelines](../../exhibitions/submission-guidelines.md) for how to contribute.

Want to curate an exhibition? Open an issue proposing your concept.
