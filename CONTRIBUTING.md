## Philosophy of Contribution

This project values:

- Diverse perspectives and experiences
- Rigorous thinking and careful argumentation
- Creative experimentation and risk-taking
- Historical awareness and theoretical grounding
- Ethical consideration of technology and power
- Accessibility and inclusive design
- Community building and collective practice

We believe that digital aesthetics emerges from conversation, debate, and shared exploration. Your voice and perspective matter.

## What We Welcome

### Essays and Theoretical Work

- Philosophical explorations of digital identity, anonymity, surveillance
- Analysis of aesthetic movements and traditions
- Cultural criticism and technology studies
- Theoretical frameworks and conceptual models
- Historical research and documentation
- Intersectional analyses (race, class, gender, disability, etc.)
- Responses to or extensions of existing essays

### Artistic Contributions

- ASCII art (original works or curated collections)
- Unicode art and symbol compositions
- Terminal interface designs
- Text-based visual compositions
- Documentation as artistic practice
- Experimental typography and layout
- Interactive or generative text art

### Manifestos and Declarations

- Personal manifestos about technology, creativity, resistance
- Collective statements from communities or groups
- Field-specific or domain-specific manifestos
- Regional or cultural manifestos
- Responses to existing manifestos

### Case Studies

- Historical examples of digital aesthetics practices
- Contemporary projects and movements
- Regional or cultural perspectives
- Accessibility-centered design examples
- Low-bandwidth or sustainable computing practices
- Underground or alternative technology movements

### Practical Resources

- Toolkits and how-to guides
- Creative exercises and prompts
- Technical tutorials
- Software recommendations
- Community building resources

### Interviews

- Conversations with practitioners, artists, activists
- Historical documentation and oral histories
- Cross-cultural dialogues

### Translations

- Translations of existing content to other languages
- Culturally adapted versions of essays and guides
- Original content in languages other than English

## What We Don't Accept

- Content promoting hate, discrimination, or harassment
- Malicious code or security exploits (even as "art")
- Violations of copyright or licensing
- Advertisements or purely promotional content
- Plagiarism or uncredited work
- Content that endangers vulnerable communities
- Bad faith arguments or trolling

## Contribution Process

### Option 1: Direct Submission (Small Additions)

For minor corrections, clarifications, or small additions:

1. Fork the repository
2. Make your changes
3. Submit a pull request with clear description
4. Respond to feedback from maintainers

### Option 2: Proposal and Discussion (Major Contributions)

For essays, manifestos, case studies, or substantial additions:

1. Open an issue describing your proposed contribution
2. Include outline or summary
3. Discuss approach with maintainers and community
4. Once approved, submit content via pull request
5. Engage with feedback and revision process

### Option 3: Collaborative Development

For large projects or collaborative works:

1. Propose project in discussions or issues
2. Form working group if others are interested
3. Create shared workspace (branch or fork)
4. Develop content collaboratively
5. Submit when ready for review

## Content Guidelines

### Writing Standards

- Clear, thoughtful prose appropriate to the genre
- Proper citations for sources and references
- Critical engagement with ideas (avoid superficiality)
- Awareness of audience and accessibility
- Original thinking and authentic voice
- Revision and editing for clarity

### Technical Standards

- Valid Markdown formatting
- Working links and references
- Properly attributed images and artwork
- Accessible formatting (headings, alt text, etc.)
- Cross-platform compatibility where possible

### Ethical Standards

- Respect for privacy and anonymity
- Awareness of power dynamics and privilege
- Inclusive language and framing
- Credit for others' work and ideas
- Consideration of harm and safety

## Essay Submission Guidelines

### Required Elements

1. Title and subtitle (if applicable)
2. Author name or pseudonym (or "Anonymous")
3. Date of composition
4. Word count (recommended 1500-5000 words, flexible)
5. Abstract or summary (100-200 words)
6. Keywords or tags
7. References and bibliography (if applicable)

### Essay Structure
```
# Essay Title
## Optional Subtitle

Author: [Name or pseudonym]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Word count: [approximate count]

## Abstract

[100-200 word summary]

## Keywords

keyword1, keyword2, keyword3

---

[Essay content]

---

## References

[Bibliography if applicable]

## Author Bio

[Optional 50-100 word bio]
```

### Content Guidance

- Begin with clear introduction and thesis
- Develop arguments with evidence and reasoning
- Engage with existing literature and ideas
- Consider counterarguments
- Conclude with implications or questions
- Write for an intelligent general audience, not just specialists

### Topics of Interest

- Philosophy of technology and digital culture
- Identity, anonymity, and privacy
- Aesthetics of constraint and limitation
- Terminal and command-line culture
- Text-based art and expression
- Surveillance and resistance
- Alternative and underground technology
- Accessibility and inclusive design
- Regional and cultural digital practices
- History of digital aesthetics

## Art and Gallery Submissions

### ASCII Art Submissions

Required information:
- Artwork (in plain text format)
- Title
- Artist name or pseudonym
- Date created
- Technique or tools used
- Artist statement (50-200 words)
- License (CC0, CC-BY, etc.)

Optional:
- Process notes or sketches
- Variations or iterations
- Technical breakdown

### Unicode Art Submissions

Same requirements as ASCII art, plus:
- Character set used
- Rendering considerations
- Cross-platform testing notes

### Terminal Interface Designs

Required:
- Screenshots or recordings
- Source code or configuration (if applicable)
- Description of design decisions
- Accessibility considerations
- License

### Documentation as Art

Required:
- Link to documentation
- Permission to feature/quote
- Analysis of aesthetic techniques
- What makes it exemplary

### Submission Format
```
# Artwork Title

Artist: [Name or pseudonym]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Technique: [Tools and methods]
License: [License type]

## Artwork

[ASCII/Unicode art here]

## Artist Statement

[50-200 words about the work]

## Technical Notes

[Optional: process, techniques, tools]
```

## Manifesto Submissions

### Manifesto Types

- Personal manifestos (individual declarations)
- Collective manifestos (group statements)
- Field-specific manifestos (disciplinary focus)
- Cultural manifestos (regional or cultural perspective)
- Responsive manifestos (replies to existing works)

### Required Elements

1. Title
2. Author(s) or collective name
3. Date
4. Manifesto text (500-3000 words recommended)
5. Context or background (optional but helpful)

### Manifesto Structure
```
# Manifesto Title

Author(s): [Name(s) or collective]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Type: [Personal/Collective/Field-specific/etc.]

## Context

[Optional: Why this manifesto? What prompted it?]

---

[Manifesto text]

---

## Signatories

[If collective: list of signatories]

## How to Sign

[If open to additional signatures: instructions]
```

### Writing Guidance

- Be bold and clear in your convictions
- Use concrete language and specific claims
- Balance vision with practicality
- Consider both critique and construction
- Invite response and dialogue

## Translation Contributions

We enthusiastically welcome translations to make this work accessible to more communities.

### Translation Guidelines

1. Request translation permission via issue
2. Maintain meaning and spirit of original
3. Adapt culturally where appropriate
4. Note where you've adapted rather than translated literally
5. Include translator's note if helpful
6. Maintain Markdown formatting
7. Test rendering in target language

### File Naming

- Original: `essay-title.md`
- Translation: `essay-title.es.md` (for Spanish)
- Use ISO 639-1 language codes

### Translator Credit

Add at top of translated document:
```
Translated by: [Name]
Translation date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Original: [link to original]
```

## Community Standards

### Respectful Engagement

- Engage ideas, not people
- Assume good faith
- Welcome diverse perspectives
- Be patient with learning processes
- Acknowledge expertise and lived experience

### Constructive Feedback

- Be specific about what works and what doesn't
- Suggest improvements, don't just criticize
- Balance positive and critical feedback
- Remember that behind every contribution is a person

### Conflict Resolution

- Address disagreements calmly
- Seek to understand before responding
- Involve maintainers if needed
- Take breaks when discussions become heated

### Enforcement

Violations of community standards may result in:
1. Warning and request for correction
2. Temporary suspension from contributing
3. Permanent ban (for serious or repeated violations)

Report violations to maintainer via email or direct message.

## Recognition and Credit

Contributors are recognized through:
- Author attribution on contributed works
- Contributor list in project documentation
- Thank you notes in release announcements
- Links to contributor websites or portfolios (if desired)

## Questions and Support

- Open an issue for questions about contributing
- Use discussions for broader conversations
- Tag maintainers if you need direct response
- Be patient with response times (volunteer project)

## License and Rights

By contributing, you agree that:
- Your contribution will be licensed under the project license (CC0 or Creative Commons, depending on content type)
- You have the right to contribute the content
- You have properly attributed any sources or references

You retain copyright to your work, but grant the project permission to use, modify, and distribute it under the project license.
