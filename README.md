# digital-aesthetics-manifesto
A manifesto that links digital anonymity, ASCII/Unicode art, terminal-native aesthetics, and philosophical reflections on technology and creativity.

Project Outline:

Write a manifesto that links digital anonymity, ASCII/Unicode art, terminal-native aesthetics, and philosophical reflections on technology and creativity
Explore how text-based media shape online identity, privacy, and artistic self-expression
Include essays, thought-experiments, historical context, and visual demonstrations (ASCII/Unicode galleries)
Provide templates for manifestos, creative exercises, and digital zines
Key Structure Sections:

Philosophy & history of digital anonymity and aesthetics
Role of text and code as artistic/identity media
Case studies (terminal art, cyphers, stylized documentation)
Manifesto templates and creative prompts for readers
Visual gallery and zine/sketchbook annex
