# Communities and Links

## Introduction

Organizations, online communities, resources, and networks that align with the values of the Digital Aesthetics Manifesto: privacy, openness, creativity, and resistance.

---

## I. Digital Rights Organizations

### Major Advocacy Groups

**Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF)**  
https://www.eff.org/  
*Founded*: 1990  
*Focus*: Digital civil liberties, online privacy, free expression

The leading digital rights organization. Legal defense, policy advocacy, and public education. Essential resource for understanding digital rights.

**Support**: Donations, membership  
**Follow**: @eff on Mastodon, Twitter

---

**American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU)**  
https://www.aclu.org/  
*Founded*: 1920  
*Focus*: Civil liberties (including digital rights)

Litigation and advocacy for civil liberties. Strong digital rights program covering surveillance, privacy, and free speech.

**Support**: Donations, membership

---

**Access Now**  
https://www.accessnow.org/  
*Founded*: 2009  
*Focus*: Digital rights globally, internet shutdowns

International organization defending digital rights worldwide. 24/7 digital security helpline for activists and journalists.

**Support**: Donations

---

**Privacy International**  
https://privacyinternational.org/  
*Founded*: 1990  
*Focus*: Privacy rights globally, surveillance

Research, advocacy, and legal action for privacy rights. Strong focus on surveillance technology and corporate accountability.

**Support**: Donations

---

**Fight for the Future**  
https://www.fightforthefuture.org/  
*Founded*: 2011  
*Focus*: Internet freedom campaigns

Digital rights activism focusing on net neutrality, surveillance, and internet freedom. Creative campaigns and direct action.

**Support**: Donations, campaign participation

---

### Free Software and Open Source

**Free Software Foundation (FSF)**  
https://www.fsf.org/  
*Founded*: 1985 (by Richard Stallman)  
*Focus*: Software freedom, GNU Project

Promotes free software and user control. Maintains Free Software Definition and GNU General Public License.

**Support**: Membership, donations

---

**Software Freedom Conservancy**  
https://sfconservancy.org/  
*Founded*: 2006  
*Focus*: Legal home for free software projects

Provides legal, infrastructure, and administrative support to free software projects.

**Projects**: Git, Inkscape, BusyBox, many others

---

**Open Source Initiative (OSI)**  
https://opensource.org/  
*Founded*: 1998  
*Focus*: Open source software promotion

Maintains Open Source Definition and approves open source licenses. More business-friendly than FSF.

---

### Security and Anonymity

**Tor Project**  
https://www.torproject.org/  
*Founded*: 2006  
*Focus*: Anonymous communication tools

Develops Tor network and Tor Browser. Essential infrastructure for privacy and circumventing censorship.

**Support**: Donations, running relays

---

**Tails**  
https://tails.boum.org/  
*Founded*: 2009  
*Focus*: Privacy-focused operating system

Live OS routing all traffic through Tor. Used by journalists and activists worldwide.

**Support**: Donations

---

**Security Without Borders**  
https://securitywithoutborders.org/  
*Founded*: 2016  
*Focus*: Security assistance for at-risk communities

Security help for journalists, activists, and nonprofits. Digital security training and incident response.

**Support**: Donations, volunteering

---

## II. Online Communities

### Thoughtful Discussion

**Tildes**  
https://tildes.net/  
*Type*: Invite-only discussion community  
*Focus*: High-quality conversation

Text-only, non-profit, privacy-respecting. Moderated for quality. No karma, no ads, no tracking.

**Join**: Request invite or get one from existing member

---

**Lobsters**  
https://lobste.rs/  
*Type*: Invite-only programming community  
*Focus*: Computing, programming

Small, technical community with invitation tree. Transparent moderation. Quality over quantity.

**Join**: Get invitation from existing member

---

**Hacker News**  
https://news.ycombinator.com/  
*Type*: Open forum  
*Focus*: Tech, startups, computing

Y Combinator's forum. High-quality technical discussion. Minimalist design. Sometimes Silicon Valley groupthink.

**Participate**: Create account, follow guidelines

### Developer Communities

**Codeberg**  
https://codeberg.org/  
*Type*: Git hosting  
*Focus*: Non-profit, community-owned

Non-profit git hosting based on Gitea. GitHub alternative. Free for public and private repos.

**Use**: Like GitHub but non-profit

---

**sourcehut**  
https://sr.ht/  
*Type*: Development platform  
*Focus*: Minimalist, efficient

Email-driven development workflow. No JavaScript required. Fast and efficient.

**Use**: Alternative development workflow

---

**GitLab**  
https://gitlab.com/  
*Type*: DevOps platform  
*Focus*: Complete CI/CD, self-hostable

Self-hostable or use gitlab.com. More features than GitHub. Open core model.

## V. Conferences and Events

### Hacker Conferences

**HOPE** (Hackers On Planet Earth)  
https://hope.net/  
*Location*: New York City  
*Frequency*: Biennial (every 2 years)

Longest-running hacker conference in US. Organized by 2600 magazine. Technical, political, cultural.

---

**DEF CON**  
https://defcon.org/  
*Location*: Las Vegas  
*Frequency*: Annual (August)

Largest hacker conference. Villages (specific topics), CTF competitions, talks, parties. More commercial than HOPE but huge.

---

**CCC** (Chaos Communication Congress)  
https://www.ccc.de/en/  
*Location*: Germany (Hamburg or Leipzig)  
*Frequency*: Annual (December)

European hacker conference. More political than DEF CON. Excellent talks on privacy, surveillance, activism.

---

### Art and Culture

**Transmediale**  
https://transmediale.de/  
*Location*: Berlin  
*Frequency*: Annual (January-February)

Festival for art and digital culture. Critical engagement with technology.

---

**Ars Electronica**  
https://ars.electronica.art/  
*Location*: Linz, Austria  
*Frequency*: Annual (September)

Festival for art, technology, and society since 1979. Prix Ars Electronica awards.

---

**ISEA** (Inter-Society for the Electronic Arts)  
https://isea-international.org/  
*Location*: Varies  
*Frequency*: Annual

International symposium on electronic art. Rotates locations globally.

---

### Academic Conferences

**ACM SIGCHI** (Human-Computer Interaction)  
https://sigchi.org/  
*Location*: Varies  
*Frequency*: Annual (April-May)

Premier HCI conference. Research on human-computer interaction.

---

**SIGGRAPH**  
https://www.siggraph.org/  
*Location*: Varies  
*Frequency*: Annual (summer)

Computer graphics and interactive techniques. Academic and industry.

## XI. Getting Involved

### Ways to Participate

**Donate**: Support organizations financially  
**Volunteer**: Offer skills (coding, writing, design, legal)  
**Run Infrastructure**: Host Tor relay, Mastodon instance, etc.  
**Create Content**: Write, make art, document, teach  
**Organize**: Start local chapter, host events  
**Advocate**: Contact representatives, support campaigns

---

### Finding Local Communities

**Meetup.com**: Search for tech, privacy, free software groups  
**Eventbrite**: Local tech events  
**University Groups**: CS departments, hackerspaces  
**Makerspaces**: Hands-on tech communities  
**Library Programs**: Many libraries have tech programs

---

### Online Participation

**Join Mastodon**: Find your community on the Fediverse  
**Contribute to FOSS**: Find projects on GitHub/Codeberg/GitLab  
**Write**: Start a blog, write for online publications  
**Make Art**: Create and share digital art  
**Teach**: Write tutorials, answer questions, mentor
