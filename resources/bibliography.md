# Bibliography and Resources

A curated collection of books, articles, websites, and media that inform and inspire the Digital Aesthetics Manifesto.

---

## Books: Technology and Society

### Critical Theory

**Shoshana Zuboff**, *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power*  
PublicAffairs, 2019  
Essential reading on how behavioral data extraction has become the dominant economic logic.

**Bruce Schneier**, *Data and Goliath: The Hidden Battles to Collect Your Data and Control Your World*  
W. W. Norton & Company, 2015  
Clear explanation of mass surveillance infrastructure and its implications.

**Cory Doctorow**, *Little Brother*  
Tor Books, 2008  
Young adult fiction that teaches surveillance resistance through narrative.

**Ursula Franklin**, *The Real World of Technology*  
House of Anansi Press, 1999  
Distinguishes between "prescriptive" and "holistic" technologies; argues for user control.

**Ivan Illich**, *Tools for Conviviality*  
Harper & Row, 1973  
Foundational text on technologies that enhance human capability without creating dependencies.

**Langdon Winner**, *The Whale and the Reactor: A Search for Limits in an Age of High Technology*  
University of Chicago Press, 1986  
"Do artifacts have politics?" Essential reading on how technology embeds values.

### Digital Culture and Identity

**Gabriella Coleman**, *Hacker, Hoaxer, Whistleblower, Spy: The Many Faces of Anonymous*  
Verso, 2014  
Ethnographic study of Anonymous, hacker culture, and online activism.

**danah boyd**, *It's Complicated: The Social Lives of Networked Teens*  
Yale University Press, 2014  
How young people navigate identity, privacy, and community online.

**Sherry Turkle**, *Life on the Screen: Identity in the Age of the Internet*  
Simon & Schuster, 1995  
Early exploration of multiple online identities and virtual selves.

**Helen Nissenbaum**, *Privacy in Context: Technology, Policy, and the Integrity of Social Life*  
Stanford University Press, 2009  
Develops "contextual integrity" framework for understanding privacy.

**Julie Cohen**, *Configuring the Networked Self: Law, Code, and the Play of Everyday Practice*  
Yale University Press, 2012  
Legal and philosophical analysis of identity, privacy, and autonomy online.

## Books: Aesthetics and Design

### Digital and New Media

**Lev Manovich**, *The Language of New Media*  
MIT Press, 2001  
Foundational theory of digital media aesthetics and computational form.

**N. Katherine Hayles**, *Writing Machines*  
MIT Press, 2002  
Materiality of text in digital environments.

**Alexander Galloway**, *Protocol: How Control Exists After Decentralization*  
MIT Press, 2004  
How network protocols shape culture and power.

**Wendy Chun**, *Programmed Visions: Software and Memory*  
MIT Press, 2011  
Software as ideology; the politics of code.

**Hito Steyerl**, *Duty Free Art: Art in the Age of Planetary Civil War*  
Verso, 2017  
Essays on art, technology, and geopolitics by influential artist and writer.

### Typography and Text

**Ellen Lupton**, *Thinking with Type: A Critical Guide for Designers, Writers, Editors, & Students*  
Princeton Architectural Press, 2004  
Comprehensive introduction to typography.

**Robert Bringhurst**, *The Elements of Typographic Style*  
Hartley & Marks, 1992  
The classic reference on typography as craft.

**Matthew Butterick**, *Practical Typography*  
Self-published, 2010-2025 (ongoing web book)  
Modern, opinionated guide. Available at: https://practicaltypography.com/

**Johanna Drucker**, *The Visible Word: Experimental Typography and Modern Art, 1909-1923*  
University of Chicago Press, 1994  
Historical study of typography as art.

### Craft and Making

**Richard Sennett**, *The Craftsman*  
Yale University Press, 2008  
Philosophy of craftsmanship and skilled practice.

**Matthew Crawford**, *Shop Class as Soulcraft: An Inquiry Into the Value of Work*  
Penguin, 2009  
Argues for hands-on work and understanding how things function.

## Books: Privacy, Security, Anonymity

**Glenn Greenwald**, *No Place to Hide: Edward Snowden, the NSA, and the U.S. Surveillance State*  
Metropolitan Books, 2014  
First-hand account of the Snowden revelations.

**Julia Angwin**, *Dragnet Nation: A Quest for Privacy, Security, and Freedom in a World of Relentless Surveillance*  
Times Books, 2014  
Journalist's investigation into personal data collection.

**Bruce Schneier**, *Secrets and Lies: Digital Security in a Networked World*  
Wiley, 2015  
Security engineering and the gap between theory and practice.

**Finn Brunton & Helen Nissenbaum**, *Obfuscation: A User's Guide for Privacy and Protest*  
MIT Press, 2015  
Strategies for resisting surveillance through data pollution and noise.

## Books: Internet History and Culture

**Katie Hafner & Matthew Lyon**, *Where Wizards Stay Up Late: The Origins of the Internet*  
Simon & Schuster, 1996  
History of ARPANET and early internet development.

**Janet Abbate**, *Inventing the Internet*  
MIT Press, 1999  
Social history of internet technology.

**Julian Dibbell**, *My Tiny Life: Crime and Passion in a Virtual World*  
Henry Holt, 1998  
Ethnography of LambdaMOO and early virtual communities.

**Whitney Phillips**, *This Is Why We Can't Have Nice Things: Mapping the Relationship Between Online Trolling and Mainstream Culture*  
MIT Press, 2015  
Study of trolling, communities, and online behavior.

Essential reading on online community dynamics.

**danah boyd & Alice Marwick**, "Social Privacy in Networked Publics: Teens' Attitudes, Practices, and Strategies"  
2011 - Oxford Internet Institute  
How young people manage privacy in social media.

**Olia Lialina**, "A Vernacular Web"  
2005 - http://art.teleportacia.org/observation/vernacular/  
Early web aesthetics and amateur creativity.

**Paul Ford**, "What Is Code?"  
*Bloomberg Businessweek*, 2015 - https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2015-paul-ford-what-is-code/  
Comprehensive, accessible explanation of software.
