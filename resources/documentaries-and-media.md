# Documentaries and Media

## Introduction

Films, videos, podcasts, and multimedia resources exploring digital culture, technology, privacy, and resistance.

---

## I. Essential Documentaries

### Surveillance and Privacy

**Citizenfour** (2014)  
Director: Laura Poitras  
Runtime: 114 minutes  
https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4044364/

Real-time documentation of Edward Snowden's decision to leak NSA documents. Winner of Academy Award for Best Documentary. Poitras was there in the Hong Kong hotel room. Essential viewing. Tense as a thriller, important as history.

**Streaming**: Various platforms (rental)

---

**The Great Hack** (2019)  
Directors: Karim Amer & Jehane Noujaim  
Runtime: 114 minutes  
https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4736550/

Cambridge Analytica scandal and weaponized data. Follows multiple perspectives including whistleblower Brittany Kaiser. Shows how personal data becomes political weapon.

**Streaming**: Netflix

---

**Nothing to Hide** (2017)  
Director: Marc Meillassoux  
Runtime: 90 minutes  
https://www.nothingtohide.com/

Documentary about privacy and surveillance. Available free online with subtitles in multiple languages. Accessible introduction to privacy issues.

**Streaming**: Free at nothingtohide.com

---

**Terms and Conditions May Apply** (2013)  
Director: Cullen Hoback  
Runtime: 79 minutes

Explores what we agree to in terms of service contracts. Shows how data is collected, sold, and used. Pre-Snowden but prescient.

**Streaming**: Various platforms (rental)

---

### Hacking and Digital Culture

**We Are Legion: The Story of the Hacktivists** (2012)  
Director: Brian Knappenberger  
Runtime: 93 minutes

History of Anonymous from 4chan to Operation Payback to Arab Spring. Interviews with participants. Shows evolution from pranks to political action.

**Streaming**: Various platforms (rental)

---

**The Internet's Own Boy: The Story of Aaron Swartz** (2014)  
Director: Brian Knappenberger  
Runtime: 105 minutes  
https://archive.org/details/TheInternetsOwnBoyTheStoryOfAaronSwartz

Biography of programmer, activist, and Reddit co-founder Aaron Swartz. Covers RSS, Creative Commons, SOPA fight, JSTOR case, and tragic death. Moving portrait of brilliant mind and broken system.

**Streaming**: Free at archive.org

---

**Downloaded** (2013)  
Director: Alex Winter  
Runtime: 106 minutes

History of Napster and file-sharing revolution. Interviews with Shawn Fanning, Sean Parker, and music industry figures. Shows how technology disrupted entrenched industry.

**Streaming**: Various platforms (rental)

---

**TPB AFK: The Pirate Bay Away from Keyboard** (2013)  
Director: Simon Klose  
Runtime: 82 minutes  
https://www.tpbafk.tv/

Documentary about Pirate Bay founders and their trial. Free culture, copyright, and resistance to content industries. Available free (naturally).

**Streaming**: Free at tpbafk.tv

---

**BBS: The Documentary** (2005)  
Director: Jason Scott  
Runtime: 8 episodes, ~5 hours total  
https://archive.org/details/BBS.The.Documentary

Epic history of bulletin board systems. Eight episodes covering technology, culture, art, phreaking, hacking. Nostalgia for pre-web internet. Available free at archive.org.

**Streaming**: Free at archive.org

---

### Free Software and Open Source

**Revolution OS** (2001)  
Director: J.T.S. Moore  
Runtime: 85 minutes

History of GNU/Linux and open source movement. Interviews with Richard Stallman, Linus Torvalds, Eric S. Raymond, Bruce Perens. Shows ideological divide between free software and open source.

**Streaming**: Free on YouTube, various platforms

---

**The Code** (2001)  
Director: Hannu Puttonen  
Runtime: 58 minutes

Finnish documentary on Linux and open source. Focus on Linus Torvalds and Finnish computing culture.

**Streaming**: YouTube

---

### Internet History

**Lo and Behold: Reveries of the Connected World** (2016)  
Director: Werner Herzog  
Runtime: 98 minutes

Werner Herzog examines internet—its history, present, and possible futures. Ten chapters covering ARPANET, hackers, trolls, AI, and Mars colonization. Herzog's distinctive philosophical narration.

**Streaming**: Various platforms (rental)

---

**Triumph of the Nerds** (1996)  
Director: Paul Sen  
Runtime: 3 episodes, 165 minutes total

PBS documentary on PC revolution. Interviews with Bill Gates, Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and others. Shows garage startup culture. Dated but historically valuable.

**Streaming**: YouTube, PBS

## II. Short Films and Video Essays

### Net Art and Digital Aesthetics

**Desktop** (2013)  
Director: Jillian Mayer  
Runtime: 1:49:38  
https://vimeo.com/65316726

Feature-length experimental film taking place entirely on a computer desktop. Screen recording as cinema. Watching someone use their computer becomes narrative.

**Streaming**: Free on Vimeo

---

**The Random Darknet Shopper** (2014-ongoing)  
Artists: !Mediengruppe Bitnik  
Documentation: Various lengths

Art project that autonomously purchased items from darknet markets and displayed them in gallery. Documentation of bot's purchases. Raises questions about agency, law, and art.

**Info**: https://wwwwwwwwwwwwwwwwwwwwww.bitnik.org/

### Explanatory Videos

**Tim Berners-Lee on the Next Web** (2009)  
TED Talk  
Runtime: 16 minutes  
https://www.ted.com/talks/tim_berners_lee_the_next_web

Web inventor discusses linked data and semantic web. Historical perspective from someone who built the original web.

**Streaming**: Free on ted.com

---

**Clay Shirky: How the Internet Will (One Day) Transform Government** (2012)  
TED Talk  
Runtime: 18 minutes

Optimistic (perhaps overly) view of internet's potential for democratic participation. Good introduction to Shirky's thinking.

**Streaming**: Free on ted.com

---

**Edward Snowden: Here's How We Take Back the Internet** (2014)  
TED Talk  
Runtime: 34 minutes

Snowden speaking via robot telepresence from Russia. Discusses surveillance, privacy, and resistance. Articulate explanation of why privacy matters.

**Streaming**: Free on ted.com

## III. Podcasts and Audio

### Long-Form Interview Podcasts

**Command Line Heroes**  
Host: Saron Yitbarek  
Red Hat production  
https://www.redhat.com/en/command-line-heroes

Podcast about people who transform technology. Episodes on operating systems, programming languages, open source. Well-produced, accessible.

**Notable Episodes**:
- "OS Wars" (Parts 1-2) - UNIX, Linux, Windows history
- "Creating JavaScript" - Brendan Eich interview
- "The Markup Languages" - HTML, XML, Markdown

---

**Darknet Diaries**  
Host: Jack Rhysider  
https://darknetdiaries.com/

True stories from dark side of internet—hacking, data breaches, cybercrime. Excellent storytelling, well-researched, suspenseful.

**Notable Episodes**:
- "The Shadow Brokers" - NSA exploit leak
- "Stuxnet" - Iranian nuclear facility attack
- "The Phone Phreaks" - Early hacker culture

---

**Note to Self** (now **IRL** with Mozilla)  
Various hosts  
https://irlpodcast.org/

Explores life online. Covers privacy, AI, misinformation, digital wellness. Public radio style, accessible.

**Notable Episodes**:
- "Is Your Device Listening?"
- "What Price Privacy?"
- "The Battle for Your Browser"

---

**Intercepted**  
Host: Jeremy Scahill  
The Intercept  
https://theintercept.com/podcasts/

Political podcast that frequently covers surveillance, whistleblowers, and digital rights. In-depth interviews and investigation.

**Notable Episodes**: Any featuring Glenn Greenwald, Laura Poitras, or surveillance state discussions

## V. Streaming Series and Shows

### Documentary Series

**How To with John Wilson** (HBO, 2020-present)  
Creator: John Wilson  
Runtime: ~30 minutes per episode

Ostensibly instructional videos become meditations on urban life and human behavior. Shot in New York pre-pandemic. Captures vernacular life. Accidentally about surveillance culture (everything is recorded).

**Streaming**: HBO Max

---

**Black Mirror** (Netflix, 2011-2019)  
Creator: Charlie Brooker  
Runtime: ~60 minutes per episode

Science fiction anthology exploring dark implications of technology. Variable quality but some episodes are brilliant social criticism.

**Essential Episodes**:
- "Fifteen Million Merits" (gamified labor)
- "White Bear" (surveillance and punishment)
- "Nosedive" (social credit)
- "San Junipero" (digital afterlife)

**Streaming**: Netflix

---

**The Undeclared War** (Peacock/Channel 4, 2022)  
Creator: Peter Kosminsky  
Runtime: 6 episodes, ~45 minutes each

British thriller about cyberwarfare and GCHQ (UK intelligence). Shows technical accuracy alongside political complexity. Surveillance, hacking, and international conflict.

**Streaming**: Peacock

---

### Narrative Films

**Her** (2013)  
Director: Spike Jonze  
Runtime: 126 minutes

Man falls in love with AI operating system. Explores loneliness, intimacy, and artificial intelligence. Beautiful cinematography. Surprisingly thoughtful about AI relationships.

**Streaming**: Various platforms (rental)

---

**Ex Machina** (2014)  
Director: Alex Garland  
Runtime: 108 minutes

Programmer tests AI for consciousness. Raises questions about sentience, exploitation, and gender. Smart, tense, visually stunning.

**Streaming**: Various platforms (rental)

---

**Snowden** (2016)  
Director: Oliver Stone  
Runtime: 134 minutes

Dramatization of Edward Snowden story. Not as good as *Citizenfour* but more accessible. Joseph Gordon-Levitt as Snowden.

**Streaming**: Various platforms (rental)

---

**The Social Dilemma** (2020)  
Director: Jeff Orlowski  
Runtime: 94 minutes

Documentary-drama hybrid about social media's impact. Interviews with former tech executives. Sometimes overwrought but effective for general audiences.

**Streaming**: Netflix

Argues we're losing user skills—understanding file systems, protocols, and how things work. Calls for preserving "general purpose" computing.

**James Bridle - "New Dark Age"** (Various)

Based on his book. Explores computational thinking, algorithmic culture, and unknowability of complex systems.

**Videos**: Various conference recordings on YouTube

NSA surveillance and Tor network. Appelbaum was WikiLeaks associate and Tor developer. Controversial figure but important talks.

## X. How to Watch Critically

### Questions to Ask

**About Surveillance**:
- Who is watching and why?
- What's invisible in this documentary?
- Who profits from surveillance?

**About Technology**:
- Who builds it and for whom?
- What's presented as inevitable that isn't?
- What alternatives aren't discussed?

**About Narratives**:
- Whose story is centered?
- What voices are missing?
- What's the implicit politics?

### Watching Practices

- **Take notes**: Pause to think and write
- **Watch with others**: Discuss and debate
- **Follow citations**: Research people and ideas mentioned
- **Make responses**: Create your own work in dialogue
